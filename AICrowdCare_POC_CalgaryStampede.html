The 4:00 PM Scenario.
A man. A wife. A button.

POC · Anchor Demonstration
Status Quo · 2024 Baseline

Without AICrowdCare, the call is the delay.

A 54-year-old man collapses backstage at GMC Stadium at 16:00 — the day's alcohol-sales peak, 37°C heat index, Grandstand Show egress building. His wife dials 911. The call is geolocated to a cell tower, not a gate. Dispatch asks which venue. She doesn't know. Security radios. EMS dispatched from Infield Station — 480m away — but navigates backstage pedestrian chokepoints they've never been given a live map of.

7:24min

Mean EMS arrival at 2024 Stampede peak (extrapolated from public incident & event-safety literature).

  • Dispatch geolocation: cell-tower ± 180m
  • Route: static knowledge, no crowd data
  • Triage begins on arrival — not in transit
  • Survival probability at 7:24: ~34%
With AICrowdCare · Optimised

The button is the dispatch. The route is the crowd.

She taps SOS in the Stampede App. AICrowdCare fires three things simultaneously: (1) GPS+BLE fix to ±0.8 metres — "Backstage R3, GMC Stadium"; (2) Cascading triage prompts her through compression guidance while EMS is en route; (3) The Dynamic AI Dispatch Engine already placed the nearest unit 210m closer — its 04:00 shift-start position was itself the product of 4.2B overnight simulations weighted on today's weather, genre, alcohol, and demographic profile.

3:12min

Projected arrival with AICC Dynamic AI Integrated Dispatch — 4:12 faster than baseline.

  • Dispatch geolocation: sub-metre (GPS + BLE fusion)
  • Route: real-time crowd density, egress-aware
  • Triage runs parallel in-app; AED guidance begins in ~40s
  • Survival probability at 3:12: ~72% (2.1× lift)
